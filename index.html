CoreML Emotion Detection IOS App

I built a real-time facial emotion detection iOS app using Apple's CoreML framework and SwiftUI. The app uses a convolutional neural network model trained on the FER2013 dataset to classify facial expressions from images captured by the phone's camera. The model achieves approximately 70% accuracy in detecting 7 emotions: angry, disgust, fear, happy, sad, surprise and neutral.

I used transfer learning with a VGGFace model pretrained on face images as a base, and added new classifier layers that I trained on the emotion dataset. The app handles image preprocessing, invoking the CoreML prediction API, and displaying results with confidence scores. Through this project, I gained experience in training machine learning models and deploying them effectively in a mobile application.

Words With Friends Solver

This Python application is designed specifically for Words with Friends and Scrabble enthusiasts: an indispensable tool that provides a competitive edge by enabling users to effortlessly identify the highest-scoring playable words on a game board. Inspired by the research of Andrew W. Appel and Guy Jacobson, renowned for their development of "The World's Fastest Scrabble Algorithm," this application implements their algorithm to deliver both speed and accuracy.

At the core of our application lies a trie created to facilitate the generation of prefixes and suffixes for each tile. This approach not only ensures efficient and fast word exploration but also takes into account various edge cases that often arise in gameplay such as handling board modifiers, special tiles, and preexisting letter flagging.

This application empowers users to gain a strategic advantage in their Words with Friends and Scrabble matches. Say goodbye to time-consuming manual word searches and hello to a streamlined and dynamic experience, where the highest-scoring playable words are just a click away. Embrace this invaluable tool and elevate your gameplay to new heights.

Wander: Travel Spontaneously

Wander is an online travel agency web application that revolutionizes the way users plan and book their trips. Assisted by generative AI, the application intelligently analyzes user preferences to curate and book personalized travel experiences.

Only 43% of US citizens hold a US passport, which is a rapidly decreasing percentage since the 70s. This is in stark contrast to the passport holding percentage of 60-70% of Canadians, and 70-80% of Europeans, Australians, and New Zealanders. Our vision is to encourage spontaneity and foster cultural understanding, allowing users to embark on unforgettable journeys tailored to their unique tastes.

Wander seamlessly integrates with the Amadeus Travel GDS and leverages various REST APIs to access a vast database of approximately 400,000 geography definitions. By dynamically filtering and selecting from over 35 different property categories and a staggering 75 million traveler ratings, Wander provides unique and tailored recommendations for each user.

Summy

Summy is a text summarization web application that allows users to summarize long texts efficiently. Users can input text of any length and Summy will generate a concise summary while preserving key information.

To enable summarization of long texts, Summy tokenizes the input into chunks that are processed through an AWS Lambda function. This serverless architecture removes restrictions on input length and allows Summy to handle large volumes of text.

By generating condensed summaries, Summy enables users to absorb information more quickly. Rather than having to read full-length texts, users can skim Summy's summaries to get the core ideas and facts. This can significantly improve reading comprehension and retention.
